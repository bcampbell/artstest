Science funding 'crucial'

Last updated 05:00 21/06/2013

Relevant offers

Two Te Atatu primary schools have been awarded grants from the first round of The Bayer Primary School Science Fund.

Tirimoana Primary School will receive $1000 which will go towards science equipment to support their science expo in term 3 and school enviro-a-thon.

Peninsula Primary School will receive $1722 which will help with equipment purchases relating to the environmental science topics and sustainability.

The fund is administered by the Royal Society of New Zealand and its chief executive Dr Di McCarthy says science support at a primary school level is crucial.

"Primary school science students explore how the natural world works so that they can participate as critical, informed and responsible citizens in a society in which science plays a significant role."

The Bayer funding allows schools to run a activities such as planting native gardens.
